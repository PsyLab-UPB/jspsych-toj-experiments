Declaration of Consent

Dear participant,

Thank you very much for participating in this study.

In the following computer-based experiment, we investigate the control of spatial visual attention and the perception of time. It is of great importance that you report your perceptions as accurately as possible or respond as accurate as possible. The exact details and instructions for the concrete task are given just before the start of the experiment.

Your participation is voluntary and you can quit at any time without stating any reasons and without facing any consequences. The data you produced until then will only be used in our evaluation if you explicitly agree,

Please be aware that these experiments require attention and mental focus and can be fatiguing. If this is the case, please take short breaks.

The recorded data will be handled confidentially and anonymously and the results of the evaluation are never brought in relation with your person.

For participating you receive a compensation of 8€ per hour, calculated on a 50¢ per 3.75 min participation basis. The compensation is calculated based on the time actually spent to work on the experimental task. The time includes breaks up to 6 min per hour, if more time is spent for breaks, it is deducted from the compensation. Oddly slow participants are paid according to a reference time based on the normal experiment duration.

In the case that you participate for course credit, no compensation is paid. Similarly, no compensation is paid if you participate as part of your job in PsyLab or if you participate without compensation on your request.

The use of electronic devices is not permitted during the experiment. This includes but is not limited to, cell phones, laptops, and mp3 players. The compensation will not be paid when malicious behavior is noticed, such as intentionally quitting the computer program during the experiment or deliberately prolonging the experiment's duration (the experimental software may monitor this).

PsyLab reserves the right to exclude participants from further experiments if malicious behavior is noticed.

If you have questions with regard to the research background or your rights as a participant, you can consult the lab staff or the author of the experiment at any time.
